ABSTRACT

Whenever the fault occurs in underground cable it is difficult to detect the exact location of the fault for process of repairing that particular cable. Diagnosing fault is difficult and entire cable should be taken out from the ground to check and fix faults. The project work is intended to detect and classify fault types with its location using neural network. To locate a fault in the cable, the cable must be tested for faults. This system uses MATLAB simulations to analyze electrical signals. The current, voltage & zero sequence would vary depending upon the length of fault of the cable. The proposed system detects the fault and classify fault types with exact location. The training, testing and evaluation are done based on Levenberg-Marquardt with back propagation algorithm. This method is tested on the IEEE-9 bus system, showing better speed and accuracy than traditional methods. This solution improves the reliability and efficiency of Power distribution networks.

Keywords: Fault detection, fault classification neural networks, MATLAB simulation, IEEE 9-bus system, Levenberg-Marquardt algorithm.

1.2. Underground Cable

4.6 Comparison with Traditional Methods

Neural network-based fault location detection in underground cables offers significant improvements over traditional methods. Traditional impedance-based methods are simple and inexpensive but suffer from moderate accuracy and reliability issues, as they are influenced by variations in cable properties and environmental conditions. Traveling wave methods and time-domain reflectometry (TDR) provide high accuracy, especially over long distances, but require complex, expensive equipment and skilled operators for data interpretation, making them less practical for widespread use.

In contrast, neural networks excel in accuracy and reliability by learning complex patterns from historical and real-time data, ensuring consistent performance across various conditions and cable types. Although the initial development of neural networks requires substantial data and expertise in machine learning, the operational costs are lower due to the ability to work with existing sensors and less specialized hardware. Neural networks are also highly adaptable, as they can be retrained with new data to improve accuracy over time, making them a scalable and cost-effective solution for modern power distribution systems. Overall, the neural network approach provides a balanced combination of high accuracy, reliability, and lower long-term costs compared to traditional methods.
