Cable Car Safety During Thunderstorm Season – Storyboard & Procedures

Executive Summary: Modern cable car systems follow strict international safety regulations and protocols[1]. Pre-storm checks include thorough inspection of cables, brakes, anchorages and grounding systems[1][2]. Throughout operation, continuous weather monitoring and wind sensing set hard limits (e.g. 50–65 km/h crosswinds)[3]. At the first sign of lightning or severe wind, the system is halted and cables are grounded to prevent damage[4]. In past incidents – such as a 2013 alpine gondola struck by lightning leaving 150 people stranded[5], or a 2026 Swiss crash in high winds[6] – swift emergency response and evacuation protocols proved essential. This storyboard outlines a concise video script detailing scene-by-scene procedures: pre-storm preparations, in-storm actions, and post-storm recovery, including realistic human elements (engineers, dispatch, passengers) and technical safeguards. All recommendations align with official standards and lessons from case studies.

Pre/During/Post-Storm Actions

Actor / Aspect Pre-Storm During Storm Post-Storm
Engineer/Operator Monitor forecasts; inspect cables, brakes, anchorage, lightning grounding[1][2]; test wind & rain sensors; review evacuation plan. Halt operation if lightning or high winds[4][3]; announce status to all cabins; maintain radio contact with dispatch and staff. Walk the line inspecting for damage; test emergency brakes and backup systems; repair or replace components as needed[1].
Passenger Guidance Inform passengers of weather risk; secure loose items; review emergency procedures (audio/video). Remain seated; use safety belts if available; follow instructions from crew/PA; avoid panic. Communicate “all-clear” and resume normal boarding; provide post-event debrief if needed.
Systems/Equipment Ensure all sensors active; check sand-brakes or de-icing as needed; verify all ground rods and lightning arrestors ready[2]. Engage emergency brakes; supply sand on rails for wet traction if applicable[8]; activate backup power for lighting & comms. Reset system alarms; log storm event data; perform preventive maintenance on caught debris or wear from storm effects.

Safety Checklist for Engineers

Engineers should always follow regulatory protocols: continuously monitor weather forecasts and storm alerts[1]; perform thorough pre-storm inspections on cables, brakes, anchors and lightning protection systems[1][2]; verify all sensors (wind, lightning, brake) and backup power sources. Before storms, clear debris from cable grooves and switches to ensure traction[9]. During storms, execute safe shutdown procedures immediately at lightning detection or high winds[4][3]. Maintain clear communication with dispatch and emergency teams at all times. After storms, inspect the entire line for damage, test emergency brakes under load, and only resume service once all systems are certified safe.

Viewer Takeaways

  • Monitor & Prepare: Watch weather forecasts and inspect critical systems before storms; be ready to halt operations when thunder rolls in[1][4].
  • Stay Calm During Storms: If caught in a storm, passengers follow crew instructions, stay seated with safety straps, and trust that engineers will ground the line and handle the situation safely.
  • Post-Storm Safety: After the storm, expert crews inspect and test everything (cables, brakes, grounding) before restarting service[2][8]. Knowing these procedures ensures that cable cars remain a safe and reliable way to travel even in bad weather.
